Date Masamune: Father of Modern Sendai

"Rectitude carried to excess hardens into stiffness; benevolence indulged beyond measure sinks into weakness."

-- Date Masamune

The Life of Date Masamune:

  • 1567 - Born in Yonezawa, Yamagata.
  • 1573 - Survives smallpox but permanently loses site in his right eye. Eventually the eye was removed, but there are conflicting legends surrounding the circumstances of the removal.
  • 1581 - Leads a successful military campaign against the Souma family.
  • 1584 - His father, Date Teramune retires and Masamune becomes daimyou.
  • 1589 - Wins the Battle of Sugiagehara and makes Tsuruga Castle in Aizu, Fukushima his main base.
  • 1590 - Toyotomi Hideyoshi seizes Odawara Castle and Masamune reluctantly joins him, eventually helping with the Korean Invasions.
  • 1604 - Masamune and 52,000 Vassals moved to Sendai, where he began the hugely prosperous transformation from what was a fishing village into modern-day Sendai.
  • 1613 - Builds the Date Maru (St. Juan Bautista) - Japan's first Western-style ship which successfully sailed to the Phillipines, Mexico, Spain, and the Vatican.
  • 1636 - dies at the age of 68.
